Grand Canyon — Full Day of Hiking
Tuesday, April 7
Grand Canyon South Rim
Early Morning (6:30 AM)

Sunrise at Mather Point

Get up early and catch sunrise — the canyon glows orange and pink at dawn. It's a short walk from the Visitor Center parking area.

7:30 AM – 1:00 PM

South Kaibab to Bright Angel Trail

The classic adventurous day hike: descend the South Kaibab Trail to Skeleton Point (6 miles round trip, 2,040 ft elevation change) for jaw-dropping panoramic views. No shade or water on this trail, so start early. For a longer option, descend to the Tonto Trail junction and loop back up via the Bright Angel Trail (~9–12 miles total).

Afternoon

Rim Trail & Geology Museum

After the big hike, stroll the paved Rim Trail between viewpoints. Stop at the Yavapai Geology Museum for context on the 2-billion-year story carved into the rock. Visit Hopi Point — one of the best overlooks on the rim.

Evening

Desert View Drive & Sunset

Drive the 25-mile Desert View Drive east along the rim, stopping at Grandview Point, Moran Point, and Lipan Point. End at the Desert View Watchtower for sunset. This drive sets you up for tomorrow's route toward Page.

⚠ Hiking safety: Bring at least 3 liters of water per person, salty snacks, and sunscreen. April temps at the rim are 50–70°F but the inner canyon is hotter. The hike OUT is always harder than the hike in.

Page, AZ — Horseshoe Bend & Lake Powell
Wednesday, April 8
Grand Canyon → Page, AZ
🚗 ~2.5 hours · 135 miles via AZ-64 & US-89
Morning (8:00 AM)

Depart Grand Canyon

Head east via Desert View / AZ-64 to Cameron, then north on US-89 to Page. Beautiful Navajo Nation landscapes along the way. Stop in Cameron for a quick stretch at the historic Cameron Trading Post.

~10:30 AM

Arrive in Page — Check in

Drop bags at the hotel. Page is a small town so everything is close together.

Midday

Horseshoe Bend

A short 1.5-mile round-trip walk from the parking lot to one of the most photographed spots in the Southwest — the Colorado River curving 1,000 feet below you in a dramatic U-shape. Parking is $10/vehicle. NPS Info →

Afternoon

Explore Lake Powell / Wahweap Overlook

Drive out to the Wahweap Overlook for views of Lake Powell, or take an easy walk along the lakeshore. If you want more adventure, book a short boat tour or kayak rental at Wahweap Marina.

Evening

Dinner in Page

Try Big John's Texas BBQ or El Tapatio for Mexican food. Page is small but has a handful of good spots. Early night — Antelope Canyon tour in the morning.

📝 Schedule note: Ken's Tours (Lower Antelope Canyon) is closed Wednesdays, so Antelope Canyon is scheduled for Thursday morning before the drive to Bryce. This gives you a relaxed afternoon at Horseshoe Bend and Lake Powell.

Antelope Canyon → Bryce Canyon
Thursday, April 9
Page, AZ → Bryce Canyon, UT
🚗 ~2.5 hours · 150 miles via US-89 (after Antelope Canyon)
Early Morning (8:00–9:30 AM)

Antelope Canyon Guided Tour

Take a guided tour of Lower Antelope Canyon via Ken's Tours ($55/person, ~1–1.5 hrs). The swirling sandstone walls and light beams are otherworldly. No bags, backpacks, or selfie sticks allowed.

Ken's Tours (Lower) → · Adventurous (Upper) →

~10:30 AM

Drive to Bryce Canyon

Head north on US-89 through stunning red rock desert, past Kanab and into Utah. The scenery transitions from desert to pine forest as you gain elevation approaching Bryce (8,000+ ft).

~1:00 PM

Check in & enter Bryce Canyon

Drop bags in Tropic or Panguitch, then head into the park. The shuttle likely won't be running yet in early April, so you can drive your own car along the scenic road.

Afternoon

Navajo Loop + Queen's Garden Trail

The signature Bryce hike: a ~3-mile combination loop that drops you into the amphitheater among the hoodoos. Descend via the Navajo Loop (through Wall Street's narrow slot), cross the canyon floor, and ascend via Queen's Garden. Moderate difficulty, 600 ft elevation change. Allow 2–3 hours.

Evening

Sunset & Stargazing at Bryce

Watch the hoodoos light up in deep orange and red at Sunset Point. Bryce is an International Dark Sky Park — if the sky is clear, stick around after dark. You can see 7,500+ stars with the naked eye. Check if the park is hosting a ranger-led astronomy program.

⚠ Book Antelope Canyon NOW! Tours sell out weeks ahead in April. Ken's Tours: $55/person for Lower Canyon. Open Mon-Tue, Thu-Sat (closed Wed & Sun). Book at lowerantelope.com →
🌡 Bryce is at 8,000+ ft elevation — expect temperatures 15–20°F cooler than the Grand Canyon. April nights can dip below freezing. Bring warm layers.
5
Zion National Park — The Narrows or Angels Landing
Friday, April 10
Bryce Canyon → Zion National Park
🚗 ~1.5 hours · 85 miles via US-89 & UT-9
Early Morning (7:00 AM)

Last look at Bryce & depart

If you're up early, catch one more sunrise at Bryce Point — it's worth it. Then pack up and head southwest toward Zion. The drive through Red Canyon on UT-12 is stunning (stop for photos at the red rock tunnels!).

~9:00 AM

Arrive in Springdale & enter Zion

Drop bags at your hotel in Springdale (the gateway town just outside Zion's south entrance). Hop on the free Zion Canyon Shuttle — private cars aren't allowed on the scenic drive in season. The shuttle runs every 7–10 minutes and stops at all major trailheads.

Morning – Afternoon

Option A: The Narrows (Top-Down or Bottom-Up)

One of the most unique hikes in the world — you wade and walk through the Virgin River in a 1,000-foot-deep slot canyon. The bottom-up route starts at the Temple of Sinawava (shuttle stop #9) and goes as far as you want (most people do 2–5 miles in). Water is typically calf-to-knee-deep in April. Rent canyoneering shoes, neoprene socks, and a walking stick from outfitters in Springdale ($30–40/person). NPS Info →

Morning – Afternoon

Option B: Angels Landing

The famous 5.4-mile round trip with 1,488 ft of elevation gain, ending with a thrilling chain-assisted scramble along a narrow ridge with 1,000-ft drops on both sides. Requires a permit ($6/person, lottery-based). Apply at recreation.gov — day-before lottery opens at 12:01 AM the day before your hike. Allow 4–5 hours round trip.

Late Afternoon

Emerald Pools or Canyon Overlook Trail

If you have energy left, the Lower Emerald Pools Trail (1.2 miles round trip, easy) is a quick waterfall walk from the shuttle. Or drive to the Canyon Overlook Trail near the east entrance tunnel (1 mile round trip) for sweeping canyon views at sunset.

Evening

Dinner in Springdale

Springdale has great dining for a small town. Try Oscar's Cafe for burgers, Whiptail Grill for Southwestern food, or Bit & Spur for upscale Mexican with live music. It's your last park-town dinner — enjoy it.

⚠ Angels Landing permits: You must win a permit lottery to hike past Scout Lookout. Apply for the day-before lottery at recreation.gov ($6/person). Results post by 4 PM the day before. If you don't get permits, The Narrows is equally incredible and needs no permit. You can also hike to Scout Lookout without a permit for amazing views.
🌊 Narrows gear rental: Book canyoneering shoe + neoprene sock + walking stick packages from Zion Outfitter or Zion Adventure Company in Springdale. ~$30–40/person. Essential in April when the water is cold (45–55°F). Zion Outfitter →

Drive Back to Vegas
Saturday, April 11
Zion → Las Vegas
🚗 ~2.5 hours · 160 miles via I-15
Morning (optional)

One more Zion moment

If you're up early, do the short Pa'rus Trail (3.5 miles, flat, paved) along the river — a peaceful morning walk with views of the Watchman and the Towers of the Virgin. Or sleep in and have a relaxed breakfast in Springdale.

~10:00 AM

Depart Zion

Head south on UT-9 to I-15, then straight down to Las Vegas. Quick, easy desert highway drive. Much shorter than the Bryce-to-Vegas route would have been.

~12:30 PM

Arrive in Las Vegas

Check into your airport hotel. Return the rental car at the LAS rental center (connected to the airport by tram). Return the car today to avoid an extra day charge on Sunday morning.

Afternoon & Evening

Last night — Vegas

You have a full afternoon and evening in Vegas! Walk the Strip, grab a nice dinner to celebrate the trip, maybe catch a show. Get to bed at a reasonable hour for your early flight. The hotel shuttle will get you to the terminal in the morning.

💡 Bonus: more Vegas time. Because Zion is only 2.5 hours from Vegas (vs. 4 hours from Bryce), you arrive earlier and get a proper last afternoon/evening in the city.

Packing Essentials

Hiking Gear

  • Sturdy hiking boots (broken in!)
  • Daypack (20–30L)
  • Trekking poles (helpful for steep trails)
  • 3L water reservoir or bottles per person
  • Headlamp (for early starts)
  • Trail snacks — bars, nuts, dried fruit

Clothing (Layers!)

  • Moisture-wicking base layers
  • Fleece or insulating mid-layer
  • Windbreaker / rain shell
  • Warm hat & gloves (for Bryce mornings)
  • Sunhat & sunglasses
  • Quick-dry pants / hiking shorts

Sun & Safety

  • SPF 50+ sunscreen
  • Lip balm with SPF
  • First aid kit (blisters, ibuprofen)
  • Electrolyte packets
  • Hand warmers (cold mornings)

Tech & Misc

  • Portable charger / power bank
  • Camera or phone with good camera
  • Car phone mount for navigation
  • Downloaded offline maps
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Dry bag for The Narrows (phone/wallet)
