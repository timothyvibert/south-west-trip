Grand Canyon — Full Day of Hiking
Tuesday, April 7
Grand Canyon South Rim
Early Morning (6:30 AM)

Sunrise at Mather Point

Get up early and catch sunrise — the canyon glows orange and pink at dawn. It's a short walk from the Visitor Center parking area.

7:30 AM – 1:00 PM

South Kaibab to Bright Angel Trail (Rim-to-Rim Connector)

The classic adventurous day hike: descend the South Kaibab Trail to Skeleton Point (6 miles round trip, 2,040 ft elevation change) for jaw-dropping panoramic views. This trail has no shade or water, so start early. For a longer option, descend to the Tonto Trail junction and loop back up via the Bright Angel Trail (~9–12 miles total depending on turnaround point).

Afternoon

Rim Trail & Geology Museum

After the big hike, stroll the paved Rim Trail between viewpoints. Stop at the Yavapai Geology Museum for context on the 2-billion-year story carved into the rock. Visit Hopi Point — one of the best overlooks on the rim.

Evening

Desert View Drive & Sunset

Drive the 25-mile Desert View Drive east along the rim, stopping at Grandview Point, Moran Point, and Lipan Point. End at the Desert View Watchtower for sunset. This drive sets you up for tomorrow's route toward Page.

⚠ Hiking safety: Bring at least 3 liters of water per person, salty snacks, and sunscreen. April temps at the rim are 50–70°F but the inner canyon is hotter. The hike OUT is always harder than the hike in.
3
Page, AZ — Horseshoe Bend & Lake Powell
Wednesday, April 8
Grand Canyon → Page, AZ
🚗 ~2.5 hours · 135 miles via AZ-64 & US-89
Morning (8:00 AM)

Depart Grand Canyon

Head east via Desert View / AZ-64 to Cameron, then north on US-89 to Page. Beautiful Navajo Nation landscapes along the way. Stop in Cameron for a quick stretch at the historic Cameron Trading Post.

~10:30 AM

Arrive in Page — Check in

Drop bags at the hotel. Page is a small town so everything is close together.

Midday

Horseshoe Bend

A short 1.5-mile round-trip walk from the parking lot to one of the most photographed spots in the Southwest — the Colorado River curving 1,000 feet below you in a dramatic U-shape. Parking is $10/vehicle (first-come, first-served — no reservation needed). NPS Info →

Afternoon

Explore Lake Powell / Wahweap Overlook

Drive out to the Wahweap Overlook for views of Lake Powell, or take an easy walk along the lakeshore. If you want more adventure, book a short boat tour or kayak rental at Wahweap Marina.

Evening

Dinner in Page

Try Big John's Texas BBQ or El Tapatio for Mexican food. Page is small but has a handful of good spots. Early night — Antelope Canyon tour in the morning.

⚠ Schedule note: Ken's Tours (Lower Antelope Canyon) is closed Wednesdays, so Antelope Canyon is moved to Thursday morning before the drive to Bryce. This actually works better — it avoids rushing and gives you a relaxed afternoon at Horseshoe Bend.

Antelope Canyon → Bryce Canyon
Thursday, April 9
Page, AZ → Bryce Canyon, UT
🚗 ~2.5 hours · 150 miles via US-89 (after Antelope Canyon)
Early Morning (8:00–9:30 AM)

Antelope Canyon Guided Tour

Take a guided tour of Lower Antelope Canyon via Ken's Tours ($55/person, ~1–1.5 hrs). The swirling sandstone walls and light beams are otherworldly. Alternatively, book Adventurous Antelope Canyon Tours for Upper Antelope Canyon. No bags, backpacks, or selfie sticks allowed.

Ken's Tours (Lower) → · Adventurous (Upper) →

~10:30 AM

Drive to Bryce Canyon

Head north on US-89 through stunning red rock desert, past Kanab and into Utah. The scenery transitions from desert to pine forest as you gain elevation approaching Bryce (8,000+ ft).

~1:00 PM

Check in & enter Bryce Canyon

Drop bags in Tropic or Panguitch, then head into the park. Note: the shuttle may not be running yet in early April, so you can drive your own car along the scenic road.

Afternoon

Navajo Loop + Queen's Garden Trail

The signature Bryce hike: a ~3-mile combination loop that drops you into the amphitheater among the hoodoos. Descend via the Navajo Loop (through Wall Street's narrow slot), cross the canyon floor, and ascend via Queen's Garden. Moderate difficulty, 600 ft elevation change. Allow 2–3 hours.

Late Afternoon

Peek-a-Boo Loop Trail (optional)

If time and energy allow, add the 5.5-mile Peek-a-Boo Loop — arguably the best hike in the park. Strenuous with 1,500 ft of elevation change. Otherwise, save this for a quick morning session on Day 5.

Evening

Sunset at Sunset Point or Inspiration Point

The hoodoos light up in deep orange and red at sunset. Bryce is also an International Dark Sky Park — if the sky is clear, the stargazing is unreal.

⚠ Book Antelope Canyon NOW! Tours sell out weeks ahead in April. Ken's Tours: $55/person for Lower Canyon, $135 for Deluxe. Open Mon-Tue, Thu-Sat (closed Wed & Sun). Book at lowerantelope.com →
🌡 Bryce is at 8,000+ ft elevation — expect temperatures 15–20°F cooler than the Grand Canyon. April nights can dip below freezing. Bring warm layers.
5
Bryce Morning & Drive to Zion
Friday, April 10
Bryce Canyon → Zion National Park
🚗 ~1.5 hours · 85 miles via UT-12 & UT-9
Early Morning (6:00 AM)

Sunrise at Bryce Point

Wake up early for one of the best sunrises you'll ever see. The first light hitting the hoodoo amphitheater is absolutely magical. Dress warm — it'll be cold.

Morning

Fairyland Loop (optional) or Scenic Drive

If the group has energy for one more Bryce hike, the 8-mile Fairyland Loop is less crowded and spectacular. Otherwise, drive the 18-mile scenic road hitting Rainbow Point, Natural Bridge, and other overlooks.

~Noon

Drive to Zion via Scenic UT-12

This drive is a destination in itself — UT-12 is one of America's most scenic byways. You'll pass through Red Canyon (stop for photos at the red rock tunnels), then connect to UT-9 through the park's east entrance and the famous Zion–Mount Carmel Tunnel.

Afternoon

Arrive in Springdale & settle in

Check into your Springdale hotel. Walk the cute main street — shops, galleries, and restaurants line the road right outside the park entrance.

Late Afternoon

Pa'rus Trail or Canyon Overlook

Ease into Zion with the flat, paved 3.5-mile Pa'rus Trail along the Virgin River, or do the short but stunning Canyon Overlook Trail (1 mile round trip) near the east entrance tunnel for panoramic views.

6
Zion — Full Day Adventure
Saturday, April 11
Zion National Park
Early Morning (6:30 AM)

Angels Landing

The iconic Zion hike — 5.4 miles round trip with 1,488 ft of elevation gain, culminating in a narrow ridge with chains and 1,000-ft drop-offs on both sides. The views from the summit are the best in the park. Allow 4–5 hours. A permit is required for the chain section.

Late Morning / Midday

Rest, refuel, and rehydrate

Head back to Springdale for lunch. Refill water, grab food, and change into water gear for the afternoon.

Afternoon (1:00 PM)

The Narrows (Bottom-Up)

Wade and hike through the Virgin River in a slot canyon — walls tower 1,000+ feet above you and the river IS the trail. The bottom-up route from the Temple of Sinawava requires no permit. Go as far as you'd like and turn around. Expect to be in knee- to waist-deep water.

Evening

Celebration dinner in Springdale

You've earned it. Oscar's Café is a local favorite. Bit & Spur is great for southwestern food with a patio and views.

⚠ Angels Landing permits — ACT NOW: The Spring 2026 seasonal lottery (covering March–May hikes) already opened on Feb 13. If you missed it, your best bet is the day-before lottery: apply on Recreation.gov between 12:01 AM and 3:00 PM MT the day before (April 10 for an April 11 hike). $6 non-refundable application fee + $3/person if you win. Note: trail maintenance may affect April 20–23. Apply at Recreation.gov → · NPS Permit Info →
🌊 The Narrows in April: Water is cold (40–50°F) from snowmelt. Rent neoprene socks, waterproof boots, and a walking stick from an outfitter in Springdale (~$32/person for the full package). Check river flow conditions before entering — the park closes The Narrows above 150 cfs or during flash flood risk.

Packing Essentials

Hiking Gear

  • Sturdy hiking boots (broken in!)
  • Daypack (20–30L)
  • Trekking poles (helpful for steep trails)
  • 3L water reservoir or bottles per person
  • Headlamp (for early starts)
  • Trail snacks — bars, nuts, dried fruit

Clothing (Layers!)

  • Moisture-wicking base layers
  • Fleece or insulating mid-layer
  • Windbreaker / rain shell
  • Warm hat & gloves (for Bryce mornings)
  • Sunhat & sunglasses
  • Quick-dry pants / hiking shorts

Sun & Safety

  • SPF 50+ sunscreen
  • Lip balm with SPF
  • First aid kit (blisters, ibuprofen)
  • Electrolyte packets
  • Hand warmers (cold mornings)

Tech & Misc

  • Portable charger / power bank
  • Camera or phone with good camera
  • Car phone mount for navigation
  • Downloaded offline maps
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Dry bag (for Narrows valuables)
