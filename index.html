Grand Canyon — Full Day of Hiking
Tuesday, April 7
Grand Canyon South Rim
Early Morning (6:30 AM)

Sunrise at Mather Point

Get up early and catch sunrise — the canyon glows orange and pink at dawn. It's a short walk from the Visitor Center parking area.

7:30 AM – 1:00 PM

South Kaibab to Bright Angel Trail

The classic adventurous day hike: descend the South Kaibab Trail to Skeleton Point (6 miles round trip, 2,040 ft elevation change) for jaw-dropping panoramic views. No shade or water on this trail, so start early. For a longer option, descend to the Tonto Trail junction and loop back up via the Bright Angel Trail (~9–12 miles total).

Afternoon

Rim Trail & Geology Museum

After the big hike, stroll the paved Rim Trail between viewpoints. Stop at the Yavapai Geology Museum for context on the 2-billion-year story carved into the rock. Visit Hopi Point — one of the best overlooks on the rim.

Evening

Desert View Drive & Sunset

Drive the 25-mile Desert View Drive east along the rim, stopping at Grandview Point, Moran Point, and Lipan Point. End at the Desert View Watchtower for sunset. This drive sets you up for tomorrow's route toward Page.

⚠ Hiking safety: Bring at least 3 liters of water per person, salty snacks, and sunscreen. April temps at the rim are 50–70°F but the inner canyon is hotter. The hike OUT is always harder than the hike in.

Page, AZ — Horseshoe Bend & Lake Powell
Wednesday, April 8
Grand Canyon → Page, AZ
🚗 ~2.5 hours · 135 miles via AZ-64 & US-89
Morning (8:00 AM)

Depart Grand Canyon

Head east via Desert View / AZ-64 to Cameron, then north on US-89 to Page. Beautiful Navajo Nation landscapes along the way. Stop in Cameron for a quick stretch at the historic Cameron Trading Post.

~10:30 AM

Arrive in Page — Check in

Drop bags at the hotel. Page is a small town so everything is close together.

Midday

Horseshoe Bend

A short 1.5-mile round-trip walk from the parking lot to one of the most photographed spots in the Southwest — the Colorado River curving 1,000 feet below you in a dramatic U-shape. Parking is $10/vehicle. NPS Info →

Afternoon

Explore Lake Powell / Wahweap Overlook

Drive out to the Wahweap Overlook for views of Lake Powell, or take an easy walk along the lakeshore. If you want more adventure, book a short boat tour or kayak rental at Wahweap Marina.

Evening

Dinner in Page

Try Big John's Texas BBQ or El Tapatio for Mexican food. Page is small but has a handful of good spots. Early night — Antelope Canyon tour in the morning.

📝 Schedule note: Ken's Tours (Lower Antelope Canyon) is closed Wednesdays, so Antelope Canyon is scheduled for Thursday morning before the drive to Bryce. This gives you a relaxed afternoon at Horseshoe Bend and Lake Powell.

Antelope Canyon → Bryce Canyon
Thursday, April 9
Page, AZ → Bryce Canyon, UT
🚗 ~2.5 hours · 150 miles via US-89 (after Antelope Canyon)
Early Morning (8:00–9:30 AM)

Antelope Canyon Guided Tour

Take a guided tour of Lower Antelope Canyon via Ken's Tours ($55/person, ~1–1.5 hrs). The swirling sandstone walls and light beams are otherworldly. No bags, backpacks, or selfie sticks allowed.

Ken's Tours (Lower) → · Adventurous (Upper) →

~10:30 AM

Drive to Bryce Canyon

Head north on US-89 through stunning red rock desert, past Kanab and into Utah. The scenery transitions from desert to pine forest as you gain elevation approaching Bryce (8,000+ ft).

~1:00 PM

Check in & enter Bryce Canyon

Drop bags in Tropic or Panguitch, then head into the park. The shuttle likely won't be running yet in early April, so you can drive your own car along the scenic road.

Afternoon

Navajo Loop + Queen's Garden Trail

The signature Bryce hike: a ~3-mile combination loop that drops you into the amphitheater among the hoodoos. Descend via the Navajo Loop (through Wall Street's narrow slot), cross the canyon floor, and ascend via Queen's Garden. Moderate difficulty, 600 ft elevation change. Allow 2–3 hours.

Evening

Sunset at Sunset Point

The hoodoos light up in deep orange and red at sunset. Bryce is also an International Dark Sky Park — if the sky is clear, the stargazing is unreal.

⚠ Book Antelope Canyon NOW! Tours sell out weeks ahead in April. Ken's Tours: $55/person for Lower Canyon. Open Mon-Tue, Thu-Sat (closed Wed & Sun). Book at lowerantelope.com →
🌡 Bryce is at 8,000+ ft elevation — expect temperatures 15–20°F cooler than the Grand Canyon. April nights can dip below freezing. Bring warm layers.
5
Bryce Canyon — Full Day Adventure
Friday, April 10
Bryce Canyon National Park
Early Morning (6:00 AM)

Sunrise at Bryce Point

Wake up early for one of the best sunrises you'll ever see. The first light hitting the hoodoo amphitheater is absolutely magical. Dress warm — it'll be cold.

Morning (8:00 AM – Noon)

Peek-a-Boo Loop Trail

Arguably the best hike in the park. 5.5 miles with 1,500 ft of elevation change, winding through hoodoo formations with constantly changing views. Strenuous but spectacular. Allow 3–4 hours.

Afternoon

Scenic Drive & Overlooks

Drive the 18-mile scenic road to Rainbow Point, the highest overlook at 9,115 ft. Stop at Natural Bridge, Agua Canyon, and Ponderosa Canyon viewpoints. Each one is a quick walk from the parking lot with jaw-dropping views.

Late Afternoon (optional)

Fairyland Loop or Mossy Cave

If the group has energy, the 8-mile Fairyland Loop is less crowded and spectacular. For something shorter, the 0.8-mile Mossy Cave Trail features a small waterfall and cave — easy and fun.

Evening

Stargazing

Bryce Canyon has some of the darkest skies in North America. On a clear night you can see 7,500+ stars with the naked eye. Head to any overlook after dark — Sunset Point and Bryce Point are great spots. Check if the park is hosting a ranger-led astronomy program.

💡 This is your most relaxed day — no driving, no packing up. Enjoy having a full day to really soak in Bryce without rushing.

Drive Back to Vegas
Saturday, April 11
Bryce Canyon → Las Vegas
🚗 ~4 hours · 270 miles via UT-12, UT-20 & I-15
Morning (optional)

One last Bryce moment

If you're up early, catch one more sunrise or do a quick walk along the rim. Otherwise, sleep in and enjoy a relaxed morning before hitting the road.

~10:00 AM

Depart Bryce

Drive west on UT-12 through Red Canyon (stop for photos at the red rock tunnels!), then south on UT-20 to I-15. The drive through southwestern Utah is gorgeous — red rocks give way to desert as you head toward Nevada.

~2:00 PM

Arrive in Las Vegas

Check into your airport hotel. Return the rental car at the LAS rental center (connected to the airport by tram). You'll want to return the car today to avoid an extra day charge on Sunday morning.

Evening

Last night — Vegas

Grab a nice dinner on the Strip to celebrate the trip, see some lights, and get to bed at a reasonable hour for your early flight. The hotel shuttle will get you to the terminal in the morning.

💡 Return car Saturday: Drop the rental car at the airport Saturday afternoon so you don't have to deal with it Sunday morning. Most airport hotels offer free shuttle service to the terminal.

Packing Essentials

Hiking Gear

  • Sturdy hiking boots (broken in!)
  • Daypack (20–30L)
  • Trekking poles (helpful for steep trails)
  • 3L water reservoir or bottles per person
  • Headlamp (for early starts)
  • Trail snacks — bars, nuts, dried fruit

Clothing (Layers!)

  • Moisture-wicking base layers
  • Fleece or insulating mid-layer
  • Windbreaker / rain shell
  • Warm hat & gloves (for Bryce mornings)
  • Sunhat & sunglasses
  • Quick-dry pants / hiking shorts

Sun & Safety

  • SPF 50+ sunscreen
  • Lip balm with SPF
  • First aid kit (blisters, ibuprofen)
  • Electrolyte packets
  • Hand warmers (cold mornings)

Tech & Misc

  • Portable charger / power bank
  • Camera or phone with good camera
  • Car phone mount for navigation
  • Downloaded offline maps
  • Reusable water bottle
